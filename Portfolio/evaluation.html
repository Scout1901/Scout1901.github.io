Evaluation & Research

Research Proposal

Description

The artifact below is a research proposal submitted for a Research Methods course. I was the sole contributor to this project. The project proposes research on the effects of combining different dissection modalities (such as virtual and physical labs) on the academic achievement of students. It includes a literature review, proposed research methods, a data analysis plan, and a timeline for the proposed research project.

Project Reflection

This artifact demonstrates an understanding of LTD goal #2 (objective 2.1) by showing the ability to collect and summarize research within educational technology and apply that theoretical knowledge to future research ideas and opportunities for learning.Building this research proposal was the first time I've truly thought through the entire research process from start to finish. Previously, I had only taken part in pieces of the research process- data collection, a literature review, some data analysis. Writing this proposal has given me a more realistic perspective of the entire research process from start to finish, and provided me with greater respect for the amount of effort that goes into getting a research study published in an academic journal. I found that narrowing down a topic was one of the most difficult aspects of this project for me. Should I complete another research proposal I will take care to let my review of literature guide my topical decisions.

Geo-Holo User Testing Report

Description:

This user testing report was completed as part of a group game design project. Our team created a prototype for a game about the fundamentals of geology for a faculty client at the University of Missouri, and this was the report on our first round of user testing. Each team member collected data, designated in the report by group member names. Our group then compiled our information together and wrote a summary of our findings together.

  • To view the report click here.

Project Reflection:

This report aligns with LTD program goal #2 (objective 2.2) by showcasing the ability to create and execute a survey for data collection and the ability to use the information gathered to improve learning outcomes in a serious game. The completion of user testing for the Geo-Holo prototype provided our team with valuable information to help improve our game for both our client and our intended users. We were able to find errors in instruction clarity, identify problems with content delivery, and gain excellent insight into the way that players experience our game. When playtesting games in the future I hope to include a larger pool of testers to receive more data to analyze. Additionally, I would like to playtest more frequently throughout the game design process.
