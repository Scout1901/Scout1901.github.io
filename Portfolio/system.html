System Assessment

Front End Analysis Projects

The artifacts below represent a portion of a semester long course in the Front End Analysis of Systems. All work was completed individually.

Needs Assessment

Description:

This needs assessment overview is a basic outline of the needs assessment procedure used to analyze an issue that presented itself at a community college in Arkansas regarding the professional communication of students with internship supervisors.

  • To view project click here.
  • To view survey click here.

Project Reflection:

This project was a great opportunity to apply class concepts to a real-world scenario. I was not aware of what a needs assessment was before completing this course. This experience taught me the incredible importance of evaluating the needs of a client before starting any work on a learning project. This particular example is a bit lacking in detail. In the future when I conduct needs assessments, I plan to expand upon the roles and responsibilities of all involved parties, delve further into the problems and needs of clients through additional information gathering, and build a clearer data collection plan.

Procedural Analysis

Description:

This artifact represents an analysis of the procedures used for Biology students to view a pre-prepared specimen under a microscope. A procedural analysis such as this might be used in curriculum development for outlining the type of content a learner should be able to perform.

  • To view project click here.

Project Reflection:

Conducting a procedural analysis turned out to be a far more in-depth process than expected. Making sure to outline all the steps the steps necessary to complete a task from the perspective of a novice learner of the topic was eye-opening. The creation of this artifact helped me to practice empathizing with learners, a skill that is necessary for quality instructional design. If I were to complete another procedural analysis such as this one I would make sure to test my results before implementing them into the curriculum to ensure that all steps are clear and nothing important was left out of the analysis.

Prerequisite Analysis

Description:

The prerequisite analysis below represents a breakdown of the knowledge, skills, and rules that would be required for an introductory Chemistry student to accomplish a higher level learning objective related to atomic structure. It depicts all lower level objectives that an instructional designer would need to include in a curriculum that seeks to help a student achieve this objective.

Project Reflection:

Creating this artifact gave me further insight into the process of curriculum design. Thinking through how a designer should align content to provide an appropriate scaffold for students to reach a higher level learning objective according to Bloom's Taxonomy was incredibly helpful. Learning the formal process of completing a prerequisite analysis will prove valuable for the design of any sort of learning product that seeks to aid the accomplishment of a complex goal.
