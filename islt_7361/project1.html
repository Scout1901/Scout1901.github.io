Project One: Digital Images

Introduction and Learning Objective

The purpose of this image is to introduce students to gecko foot structure and function. This image is the digital image portion of my final project for Introduction to Digital Media ISLT 7361. The final digital media product will teach students about gecko foot structures and how they enable geckos adhere to surfaces.

Image Learning Objective

Students will be able to identify the key structures of gecko feet when given a sample photo of a gecko’s foot.

Design Elements

I used contrast in my learning diagram by making sure that the text, images, and other shapes were easy to read against the background I chose. I also demonstrated contrast by ensuring that my title was significantly bigger in font size then the other diagram text. I utilized repetition by using a consistent font, arrow style, and by using repeating colors throughout (green and black). Alignment was accounted for by making sure that the title was center aligned, that my vector image text was aligned properly with the shape of the raster image, and that the gecko images were aligned with the same orientation. I used proximity in my learning diagram by ensuring that each label was located closely to the corresponding image and by making sure that any subtext was in close proximity to it’s parent text. These design elements support my student learning outcome by ensuring that the labels were clear, readable, and attached to the correct part of the image. This will help students to easily and quickly identify the key parts of gecko foot anatomy.

Reflection

Completing this project helped me to gain a better understanding of the role of digital images in education. I had never tried to tie a digital image to a learning objective before this project. I had used images as supplements to teaching, but never as the basis for meeting a specific learning objective. I think that the process of learning to stop and think through an objective before beginning a project will prove invaluable in completing any future digital media work for learning. It helps provide a grounding and purpose for your digital work that I wouldn’t have developed otherwise.
Completing this project also helped me to learn the distinction between raster and vector images, and when to use each. I am looking forward to taking the raster and vector techniques I learned in Photoshop and Illustrator throughout these modules to develop more digital images! The basic image manipulation skills, the design principle overviews, and learning objective creation involved with this project helped me to understand just how powerful a well designed digital image can be at conveying information to learners.
