Project Two: Digital Audio

Introduction and Learning Objective

The purpose of this audio is to introduce students to factors that enable geckos to adhere to surfaces. This audio file is the digital audio portion of my final project for Introduction to Digital Media ISLT 7361. The final digital media product will teach students about gecko foot structures and how they enable geckos to adhere to surfaces.

Audio Learning Objective

Students will be able to identify factors that enable geckos to adhere to surfaces when given several possible options.

Licensing

Techniques

  • Capture Original Audio
  • Add Tracks
  • Use Time Shift Tool
  • Cut Using Selection Tool
  • Add Fade In/Out
  • Apply Noise Reduction Effect

Reflection

Completing this digital audio project helped me to think about the importance of clarity in audio used for education. I learned through recording myself that my words are not always as clear as I intended them to be. This can be a huge issue when trying to use audio alone to help students meet a learning objective. A few skills that I was pleased to learn were how to add effects to audio and how to splice different pieces of audio together. Knowing how to add effects, fix background noise, as well as fade in and out gives audio a polished sound that helps remove distractions to make meeting learning objectives easier. My project supports my learning objective effectively because it provides all of the necessary information students need to be able to complete the desired end task. However, when combined with video in my final project, I think the effectiveness and depth of learning will increase. Overall, this project was an excellent way to get an introduction to recording and manipulating audio clips. I hope to learn more about additional effects and filters in the future, in order to improve the quality of audio projects.
